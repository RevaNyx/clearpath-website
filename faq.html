A quick note before you dive in

Not every business needs a large, expensive website right away. Sometimes a strong landing page is enough to help people understand what you do, trust your business, and reach out. The right option depends on your goals—this FAQ helps you understand the difference.

What is a landing page?

＋
A landing page is a single page website designed to present your business clearly in one place. It usually includes your main message, services, contact information, and a call to action. It is a strong option for businesses that want to get online quickly and professionally without needing multiple pages right away.

What is the difference between a landing page and a full website?

＋
A landing page keeps everything on one page, which makes it simple and direct. A full website includes multiple pages, such as Home, About, Services, Contact, and more. If your goal is to get something polished online quickly, a landing page can be a smart starting point. If your business needs more depth, more content, or a broader online presence, a full website is usually the better fit.

How do I know which option is right for me?

＋
If you mainly need to explain what you do, show a few services, and make it easy for people to contact you, a landing page is often enough. If you want separate pages for different services, more room for content, stronger long-term growth, or a more complete brand presence, a full website makes more sense.

Why does e-commerce cost more than a full website?

＋
E-commerce websites require more setup and more responsibility. They include product listings, payment processing, checkout flows, customer actions, and rigorous testing. An e-commerce website is both informational and functional, which is why it takes more time to build.

What is the payment plan?

＋
The payment plan is a lower upfront option for clients who want a full website without paying the full cost all at once. It starts with a down payment, followed by monthly payments until the agreed total is paid off.

Timelines & Process

How long does a project take?

＋

Landing Pages: 2 to 5 business days after receiving content.

Full Websites: 1 to 3 weeks depending on complexity.

E-commerce: 2 to 4 weeks or more depending on item count and setup.

Do I need to provide my own content?

＋
If you have text, photos, or branding prepared, that helps speed things up. If not, I can still help structure the page and organize your content based on the information you provide. You do not need everything perfectly written to start.

Will my website work on mobile?

＋
Yes. Every website I build is responsive, meaning it will adjust for phones, tablets, and desktops. Most customers will see your site from their phone first, so this is non-negotiable.

Can I upgrade later?

＋
Absolutely. Starting with a landing page is a practical way to establish your online presence first and expand later as your business grows.
