Frequently Asked Questions.

If you are comparing options, trying to understand pricing, or wondering what type of website makes the most sense, this page is here to make the process clear.

The Philosophy

Not every business needs a large, expensive website right away. Sometimes a strong landing page is enough to help people understand what you do, trust your business, and reach out.

The right option depends on your goals, and this FAQ is meant to help you understand the difference so you can make the best decision for your business.

What is a landing page?

＋
A landing page is a single page website designed to present your business clearly in one place. It usually includes your main message, services, contact information, and a call to action. It is a strong option for businesses that want to get online quickly and professionally without needing multiple pages right away.

What is the difference between a landing page and a full website?

＋
A landing page keeps everything on one page, which makes it simple and direct. A full website includes multiple pages, such as Home, About, Services, Contact, and more. If your goal is to get something polished online quickly, a landing page can be a smart starting point.

Why does e-commerce cost more?

＋
E-commerce websites require more setup and more responsibility on the development side. They often include product or service listings, payment processing, checkout flows, customer actions, and testing to make sure everything works smoothly.

What is the payment plan?

＋
The payment plan is a lower upfront option for clients who want a full website without paying the full cost all at once. It starts with a down payment, followed by monthly payments until the agreed total is paid off.

Timeline & Details

How long does each option take?

＋

Landing Page: 2 to 5 business days.

Full Website: 1 to 3 weeks.

E-commerce: 2 to 4 weeks or more.

Do I need to provide my own content?

＋
If you already have text, photos, or branding, that helps. If not, I can help structure the page and organize your content based on the information you provide. You do not need to have everything perfectly written before getting started.

Will it work on mobile?

＋
Yes. Every website I build is designed to be responsive, which means it will adjust for phones, tablets, and desktops.

How do I get started?
