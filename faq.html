Frequently Asked Questions

If you are comparing options, trying to understand pricing, or wondering what type of website makes the most sense for your business, this page is here to make the process clear.

A quick note before you dive in

Not every business needs a large, expensive website right away. Sometimes a strong landing page is enough to help people understand what you do, trust your business, and reach out. Other times, a full website or an e-commerce setup makes more sense. The right option depends on your goals, and this FAQ is meant to help you understand the difference so you can make the best decision for your business.

What is a landing page?

A landing page is a single page website designed to present your business clearly in one place. It usually includes your main message, services, contact information, and a call to action. It is a strong option for businesses that want to get online quickly and professionally without needing multiple pages right away.

What is the difference between a landing page and a full website?

A landing page keeps everything on one page, which makes it simple and direct. A full website includes multiple pages, such as Home, About, Services, Contact, and more. If your goal is to get something polished online quickly, a landing page can be a smart starting point. If your business needs more depth, more content, or a broader online presence, a full website is usually the better fit.

How do I know which option is right for me?

If you mainly need to explain what you do, show a few services, and make it easy for people to contact you, a landing page is often enough. If you want separate pages for different services, more room for content, stronger long-term growth, or a more complete brand presence, a full website makes more sense. If you are unsure, I can help point you in the right direction based on your business goals.

Why does an e-commerce website cost more than a full website?

E-commerce websites require more setup and more responsibility on the development side. They often include product or service listings, payment processing, checkout flows, customer actions, and testing to make sure everything works smoothly. A standard full website is mostly informational. An e-commerce website is both informational and functional, which is why it takes more time to build and is priced higher.

What is the payment plan?

The payment plan is a lower upfront option for clients who want a full website without paying the full cost all at once. It starts with a down payment, followed by monthly payments until the agreed total is paid off. This option can make it easier to move forward now instead of waiting until the full amount is available upfront.

How long does a landing page usually take?

Most landing pages are typically completed within 2 to 5 business days after I receive the content, details, and any images needed for the project. The clearer the information provided, the smoother the process usually is.

How long does a full website usually take?

Full websites usually take around 1 to 3 weeks depending on the number of pages, the amount of content, and the overall scope. Simpler sites move faster. More detailed sites with additional sections or custom needs naturally take longer.

How long does an e-commerce website usually take?

E-commerce and online ordering websites usually take around 2 to 4 weeks or more depending on how many items, services, or purchasing steps need to be built into the site. These projects require more setup, testing, and structure than a standard business website.

Do I need to provide my own content?

If you already have text, photos, branding, or business information prepared, that helps move things along faster. If not, I can still help structure the page and organize your content based on the information you provide. You do not need to have everything perfectly written before getting started.

Will my website work on mobile devices?

Yes. Every website I build is designed to be responsive, which means it will adjust for phones, tablets, and desktops. That matters because many customers will first see your site from their phone.

Can I start with a landing page and upgrade later?

Yes. Starting with a landing page can be a practical way to establish your online presence first and expand later as your business grows. This works well for businesses that want to move quickly now and build more over time.

How do I get started?

You can start by reaching out through the contact form on the main site. Once your project is confirmed, I will guide you through the next steps, including deposit details and the intake process so I can begin building your site.
